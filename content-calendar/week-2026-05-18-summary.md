### Reel 2 — The Science
**Pillar:** The Science
**Post day:** Friday
**Hook:** `1 second versus 3 seconds. Same rep. Different results entirely.`
**CTA:** Save this — and test it this week. You'll feel the difference by set two.

---

**SCRIPT (copy-paste for HeyGen):**

1 second versus 3 seconds. Same rep. Different results entirely.

The part I'm talking about is the lowering — the eccentric phase. When your muscle lengthens under load, it generates more mechanical tension than the lift itself.

Research suggests eccentric-focused training can build up to 25% more strength than standard lifting, with less joint stress.

After 40, this matters even more. Tendons adapt more slowly than muscle. Slow eccentrics force that tendon adaptation and build collagen in connective tissue — exactly what keeps you training without injury.

The change is simple: take 3 seconds to lower any movement. Same workout. Completely different stimulus.

Save this — and test it this week. You'll feel the difference by set two.

### Carousel 1 — Actionable Tips
**Post day:** Saturday
**Title (Slide 1):** 5 signs your recovery is falling behind (and what to do about each)

---

**SLIDES (copy-paste for Gamma):**

**Slide 1**
5 signs your recovery is falling behind (and what to do about each) →

**Slide 2**
1. Resting heart rate is 5+ bpm above your baseline. → Rest day or Zone 1 walk only. No exceptions.

**Slide 3**
2. HRV is 10%+ below your 7-day average. → Prioritise sleep. Cut training intensity for 48 hours.

**Slide 4**
3. You feel worse mid-workout than at the start. → Stop. Finishing this session is counterproductive.

**Slide 5**
4. Sleep quality drops despite a normal bedtime. → Recovery debt is building. Deload for 2–3 days.

**Slide 6**
5. Motivation to train is unusually low. → Not laziness. Low drive is a genuine physiological readiness signal.

**Slide 7**
1–2 signs? Adjust today's session. 3 or more? Take 48 hours off. This is how adaptation works.

**Slide 8**
Save this. Recovery signals exist to protect your progress — not slow it down. 💚

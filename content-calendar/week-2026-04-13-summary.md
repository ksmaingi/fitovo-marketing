### Reel 3 — The Wake-Up Call
**Pillar:** The Wake-Up Call
**Hook:** `Bone density is like a savings account. You can only make deposits until your mid-30s. After that, it's withdrawals only.`
**CTA:** Save this and share it — most people need to hear this before their 50s.
**Post day suggestion:** Wednesday

---

**SCRIPT (copy-paste for HeyGen):**

Bone density is like a savings account. You can only make deposits until your mid-30s. After that, it's withdrawals only.

Peak bone mass is reached somewhere between 25 and 35. After that, you gradually lose density — faster in women after menopause, but measurable in everyone.

And here's why this should matter to you now, not at 70: every fracture risk, every fall, every 'I can't do that anymore' moment in later life is partly a bone density story written decades earlier.

Almost nobody is training for it. Because the consequences are invisible until they're not.

The prescription is actually the same as for muscle: progressive resistance training. Bone responds to mechanical stress by laying down new density. That's established physiology — it's not a theory.

Here's the thing that often gets missed: the training that protects your muscle also protects your bone. You don't need a separate programme or more sessions. You just need to be doing the right things consistently — in your 40s and 50s, when there's still meaningful margin.

Save this — and send it to someone who still thinks strength training is optional.

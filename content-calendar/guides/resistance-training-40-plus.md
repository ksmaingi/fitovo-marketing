# The Over-40 Resistance Training Guide
**By Fitovo**

You commented STRONG — so here's the full breakdown.

This is a simple, evidence-based resistance training plan designed for adults in their 40s and 50s. No gym required for the beginner version. Progressive. Sustainable. Built around the science of what actually keeps you strong and functional for decades.

---

## Why resistance training matters after 40

- **Sarcopenia** (age-related muscle loss) takes 3–5% of your muscle mass per decade after 30
- By your 60s, you could have lost 20% of your peak muscle
- Muscle regulates blood sugar, protects joints, and prevents falls
- Falls are the leading cause of injury-related death in over-65s — almost all preventable with sufficient strength
- The research consistently points to **2–3 resistance sessions per week** as the minimum effective dose

---

## The programme

### Frequency
- **3 sessions per week** (e.g., Monday / Wednesday / Friday)
- Minimum 48 hours between sessions for recovery
- If you can only manage 2 sessions, that still delivers significant benefit

### Structure
Each session follows the same template:

1. **Warm-up** — 5 minutes light movement (walk, marching on the spot, arm circles)
2. **Main exercises** — 4–5 movements, 3 sets each
3. **Cool-down** — 2–3 minutes gentle stretching

### Rest between sets
- 60–90 seconds for bodyweight exercises
- 90–120 seconds for weighted exercises

---

## Session A — Lower body focus

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Key cue |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Split squat | 3 × 8 per side | Back knee toward floor, slowly |
| Hip hinge | 3 × 10 | Push hips BACK, not down |
| Glute bridge | 3 × 12 | Squeeze at the top, hold 2 seconds |
| Calf raise (single leg) | 3 × 12 per side | Slow on the way down |
| Dead hang | 3 × 20–30 seconds | Relax your shoulders, breathe |

## Session B — Upper body focus

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Key cue |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Push-up (or incline push-up) | 3 × 8–12 | Elbows at 45°, full range |
| Inverted row (under a table) | 3 × 8–10 | Pull chest to the edge |
| Pike push-up | 3 × 6–8 | Hips high, head between arms |
| Plank | 3 × 30–40 seconds | Squeeze glutes, don't sag |
| Dead hang | 3 × 20–30 seconds | Build toward 45 seconds over time |

## Session C — Full body

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Key cue |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Split squat | 3 × 8 per side | Alternate with Session A stance |
| Push-up | 3 × 8–12 | Scale to your level |
| Hip hinge | 3 × 10 | Feel hamstrings load |
| Inverted row | 3 × 8–10 | Straight body, pull with back |
| Single-leg glute bridge | 3 × 8 per side | Keep hips level |

## Progression

The key to long-term gains is **progressive overload** — gradually making the exercises harder over time.

**Weeks 1–4:** Learn the movements. Focus on form. Use the rep counts above.

**Weeks 5–8:** Add reps. Aim to increase by 1–2 reps per set where you can.

**Weeks 9–12:** Add difficulty:
- Split squat → Rear-foot-elevated split squat (back foot on a chair)
- Push-up → Decline push-up (feet on a step)
- Hip hinge → Single-leg Romanian deadlift
- Glute bridge → Single-leg glute bridge (if not already)
- Dead hang → Increase to 45–60 seconds

**Beyond 12 weeks:** Consider adding external load (dumbbells, resistance bands, kettlebell) or progressing to a gym-based programme.

---

## Recovery

- **Sleep:** 7–9 hours. This is when your muscles repair and grow. Non-negotiable.
- **Protein:** Aim for 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight daily. Spread across meals.
- **Rest days:** Your body adapts during recovery, not during the workout. Honour the rest day.
- **HRV:** If you have an Apple Watch, check your HRV in the morning. Low HRV = go easy or rest. High HRV = green light for a harder session.

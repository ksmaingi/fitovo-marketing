## Executive Summary

Fitovo launches on the App Store in early May 2026. As of today (2026-04-13, Week 3 of a 5-week pre-launch) Instagram has **12 followers**, TikTok is being rebuilt from zero, and there is no website beyond a Carrd waitlist page. The product is differentiated: **readiness-based training (HRV + RHR + sleep → daily green/amber/red score) built specifically for adults 40–65** — a genuinely under-served segment. The brand voice is a "confident friend who happens to be a sports scientist" — warm, evidence-backed, never preachy.

Social media has three compounding jobs: (1) **build the waitlist** through Week 5, (2) **establish Kulvinder as the trustworthy founder-expert** adults over 40 want guiding their training, and (3) **create a content flywheel** that survives launch and becomes the dominant acquisition channel for the first 6 months post-launch.

**Platform focus:** Instagram primary (Reels-led, Stories daily), TikTok secondary (cross-post + founder-native content), LinkedIn experimental (building-in-public thought leadership). We are **not** pursuing Facebook, YouTube long-form, or X/Twitter in the first 6 months — resources are too thin to do them well.

**Content volume (calibrated to a 3-hour-per-week founder budget):** 2 Reels + 1 Carousel + ~5 Stories per week (Instagram) + 2 cross-posts per week (TikTok) = **~10 pieces of content/week**. Pipeline: Claude scheduled task → HeyGen (Mon batch-render) → Descript for captions → Gamma for carousels → Later for scheduling. No CapCut. No outside help.

**The 3-hour rule:** if a week's marketing work would exceed 3 hours, cut something. Burnout is a bigger enemy than under-posting. Parent + full-time other job is the constraint; everything is scaled to it.

**Expected outcomes at 3 hrs/week:**
| Milestone | Date | IG Followers | Waitlist / Users |
|---|---|---|---|
| Today | 2026-04-13 | 12 | ~5 (est.) |
| Launch day (realistic) | 2026-05-04 | **50** | **250–300** (paid-ads carry the gap) |
| Month 3 post-launch | 2026-08-04 | 800–1,000 | 500–800 paying |
| Month 6 post-launch | 2026-11-04 | 2,500–3,500 | 1,500–2,500 paying |
| Month 12 post-launch | 2027-05-04 | 8,000–10,000 | 5,000–7,000 paying |

**Why the downward revision from the first draft:** The first version of this plan assumed 10–12 hours/week of founder time. Reality is 3. After 3 weeks of the original plan, only 5 Reels + 2 Carousels actually shipped (not 15 Reels + 6 Carousels), and median reach was 148 — healthy for a new account, but there's no way to cover the shortfall organically at 3 hrs/week. Paid ads (~$500 through launch) do the work that founder-time engagement can't.

**Key focus areas:**
1. **Reels-first on Instagram, low-volume / high-signal** — 2 Reels/week, protected by big engagement windows, beats 5/week shipped into silence
2. **Founder story as a competitive moat** — nobody else building longevity tech for this demo has a founder building in public with this tone
3. **Evidence-as-marketing** — every Reel cites a real study; that's what this audience trusts
4. **Paid ads carry the organic gap** — ~$500 through launch. Meta Lead Gen → Carrd waitlist. Non-negotiable given the time budget.

## 1. Social Media Goals & Metrics

### Primary Goals (ranked)

**Goal 1: Waitlist Growth → Launch-Day Download Velocity** (Pre-launch → Week 8 post-launch)
- **Target:** Minimum 150 waitlist signups by 2026-05-04, stretch 300. Convert 60%+ to App Store downloads in Week 1 post-launch.
- **Why this matters:** App Store ranking algorithms favour velocity in the first 72 hours. A large, warm waitlist compresses launch-week installs into a narrow window and can push Fitovo into Health & Fitness category ranking that drives organic discovery for weeks.
- **How social helps:** ManyChat "Comment READY → DM waitlist link" on every Reel (already live). Instagram Story polls asking "Want early access?" Every founder Reel ends with a waitlist CTA.

**Goal 2: Thought Leadership — Own "Longevity Training After 40"** (ongoing)
- **Target:** 1 of every 3 UK/US posts in the hashtag `#fitnessafter40` tagged with `#fitovo` or linking back by Month 6. Become a recognised face in the niche.
- **Why this matters:** In a fitness market dominated by 25-year-old aesthetics content, the 40–65 segment has no dominant voice. A niche this under-served can be owned by the first founder who shows up consistently with real science and real empathy. This is the most durable differentiator we have.
- **How social helps:** The Science and Actionable Tips pillars (45% of all content) function as compounding thought-leadership assets. Every saved post is permanent distribution. Every "Save this" CTA is a future re-exposure.

**Goal 3: Community + Lead Generation Engine** (post-launch, compounding)
- **Target:** By Month 6 post-launch, social drives 40%+ of new user signups (CAC < $5 blended). Response rate to DMs >95% within 4h during business hours.
- **Why this matters:** Fitovo's unit economics only work if social and organic dominate acquisition. Paid ads can't scale this niche cheaply past a certain point; community-led acquisition through founder-led content can.
- **How social helps:** Every post is a conversation starter, not a broadcast. Reply to every comment inside 2h. Convert engaged commenters to DM relationships, DM relationships to users, users to advocates.

## 2. Target Audience & Platform Selection

### Primary Persona: "Dan at 52"

Male or female, 45–62, professional or post-professional, household income £60k+ / $100k+. Already owns an Apple Watch (or considering one). Trained in their 20s and 30s, fell off in their 40s with work/family, is now watching a parent decline and thinking about the next 30 years. Wants to be strong enough to pick up a grandchild at 75 and lucid enough to enjoy it at 85. Distrusts "biohacker" content and mainstream fitness content equally — one is cringe, the other is irrelevant.

**Social behaviour:**
- **Instagram (daily):** 30–45 min/day. Reels have replaced most of their Facebook use. They do NOT post often themselves — they consume, save, and occasionally share to spouse/friend.
- **TikTok (growing):** Often have the app but use it less intentionally. Comfortable with video, allergic to influencer aesthetics.
- **LinkedIn (professional/weekly):** Scroll during workday. Strong signal for professional credibility; weak signal for fitness content specifically.
- **Facebook:** Fading. Mostly family photos and local groups. Not a discovery channel for new brands.
- **YouTube:** Secondary — for long-form education when a Reel sparked curiosity. Opportunity later, not now.
- **Twitter/X:** Minimal overlap with target. Do not pursue.

**When active:** Weekday mornings (6–8 AM), lunch (12–1 PM), evenings (8–10 PM). Weekend mornings. Pre-bed scroll (10–11 PM) is high-save, low-comment.

**Content preferences:** Short-form video with a clear stat or claim in the first 3 seconds. Evidence (studies, named researchers) trusted more than personality. Skeptical of "10x your X" framing. Responds to practical + actionable + non-condescending.

**What they follow:** Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman (with reservations), Mary Claire Haver (menopause), Stacy Sims, Gabrielle Lyon, Vonda Wright, Don Saladino. Less: FitTok influencers, bodybuilders.

## 3. Platform-Specific Strategies

### 3.1 Instagram (Primary)

**Why:** Highest audience density for 40–65 adults on short-form video. Reels algorithm still actively rewards new accounts that nail retention + saves. Story stickers (polls, questions, quiz) are the most efficient qualitative research tool on the internet for this audience.

**Content mix (calibrated to 3 hrs/week):**
| Format | Weekly volume | % of effort | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reels (15–60s) | **2** | 50% | Primary reach driver. Low volume = better per-post support (comments, engagement window) |
| Stories (lightweight) | **5** (1/weekday, phone + text overlay) | 10% | Retention of existing followers feeds the algorithm |
| Carousels (Gamma-generated) | **1** | 25% | Highest save rate; Gamma keeps this low-friction |
| Static feed posts | 0 | 0% | Skip unless major announcement |
| Lives | Launch week + monthly post-launch | 15% | Founder connection — but batch, don't commit weekly |

**Posting schedule (scaled to actual capacity):**

| Day | Time (local) | Format | Pillar |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | — | Claude agent generates calendar 7 AM. Founder reviews evening. | — |
| Tuesday | 7:30 AM | Reel #1 | Pick 1 of: Wake-Up Call, Science, or Founder Story |
| Wed–Thu | — | 1 Story/day · quick phone + text overlay | — |
| Friday | 7:30 AM | Reel #2 | Different pillar from Tuesday (rotate) |
| Saturday | 9:00 AM | Carousel (Gamma) | Science or Actionable |
| Sun | — | Week-wrap Story thread (optional, only if time) | — |

Posting time rationale: 7:30 AM hits the pre-work scroll window; 9 AM Saturday catches the weekend coffee scroll. Later auto-publishes everything that's been pre-scheduled by Tue evening.

**Best practices locked in:**
- Hook in first 3 seconds — a scroll-stopping stat, question, or reversal
- Every Reel ends with a specific CTA (Comment trigger word / Save / Follow / Share — never more than one)
- Captions structured: hook line → body with line breaks → CTA → 10–15 hashtags (mixed volume: 5 high / 5 mid / 5 niche)
- No watermarks when cross-posting to TikTok
- Tag every study source in the caption (builds credibility)
- HeyGen avatar for "science" and "wake-up" Reels; founder-on-phone for "founder story" Reels (different energy)

**Don't:**
- Don't post product pitches without an educational hook first
- Don't use more than 15 hashtags
- Don't boost posts inside the first 24 hours (let organic signal mature)
- Don't reply to trolls publicly beyond once — take to DM or ignore

### 3.2 TikTok (Secondary)

**Why:** The 40–65 TikTok cohort is small but fast-growing, less saturated with fitness content, and highly receptive to "raw" founder-on-camera content. A single viral TikTok in this niche can deliver more reach than 3 months of Reels.

**Strategy:** Cross-post the top 3 Reels/week (no IG watermark, re-cut for TikTok pacing and captions) + 1 native TikTok-first piece/week (more raw, founder-on-phone, no HeyGen avatar). Aim for 3–4 posts/week.

**TikTok-native differences:**
- Captions: more casual, lowercase, no hashtags overload (5 max)
- First-frame hook must be even faster (TikTok retention benchmark is 1.5s, not 3s)
- Lean into trending sounds where pillar fits (rare, but powerful when it lands)
- Founder-on-phone content outperforms AI-avatar content here

**Posting cadence:** Monday / Wednesday / Friday / Sunday (if bonus content). Evening posts (6–9 PM local) perform better on TikTok than mornings for this demo.

### 3.3 LinkedIn (Experimental)

**Why:** Not where the customer buys, but where industry credibility is built. Building-in-public posts about the app + the longevity market can attract: investors, advisors, potential hires, podcast invites, and earned media journalists. Low cost, high asymmetric upside.

**Strategy:** 2 founder posts/week from Kulvinder's personal profile (not a Fitovo page — personal profiles get 10× the reach for thought leadership on LinkedIn). No company page until post-launch.

**Topics:**
- The business case for longevity apps
- What I learned shipping an iOS app solo
- The 40–65 fitness market is bigger than you think
- Specific product design decisions and why

**Format:** Text + 1 image (no multi-image carousels — lower reach on LinkedIn). Long-form (~200 words) outperforms short.

### 3.4 Channels we are NOT pursuing (and why)

- **Facebook:** Audience is there but posting here signals "not for me" to younger cohorts we'll want eventually. Revisit at M6+.
- **YouTube (long form):** Needs different production stack, too expensive pre-launch. Revisit at M6 with founder vlogs.
- **X/Twitter:** Our audience isn't here. Our competitors aren't here. Don't dilute.
- **Pinterest:** Would work for carousel content but requires a separate design pipeline. Revisit at M9+.

## 8. Content Production Workflow

This is the section `docs/marketing-plan.md` already covers in detail (Section 6, "Claude Scheduled Agent"). Operational reference lives there. Strategy-level workflow below.

### Weekly cadence (calibrated to 3 hrs/week, DIY, no outside help)

| When | Activity | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Mon 7 AM | Claude scheduled task auto-generates calendar (2 Reels + 1 Carousel + 5 Stories) | 0 (async) |
| Mon evening | Review Claude output, approve/tweak scripts, record voice for both Reels, **submit all HeyGen renders in a parallel batch** | 30 min |
| Tue morning | Pick up finished HeyGen output, Descript touch-up, Gamma carousel (paste outline → export), schedule all in Later for the week | 30 min |
| Weekdays ×5 | 10-min engagement block: post Story, reply to own comments, 3–5 thoughtful comments on target accounts | 50 min |
| Sun evening | 15-min weekly review — what shipped, reach, saves, what to adjust | 15 min |
| **Total** | | **~2h 15m · ~45 min buffer** |

### The HeyGen batch-render rule

Submit both Reels' voice + script inputs on Monday morning in one session. Creator-plan queue handles parallel renders. Walk away — finished output returns 2–4 hrs later. **This is the single biggest time-saver — eliminates sequential waiting.** Never submit one, wait, edit, submit another. Batch always.

### Approval gate

Kulvinder is the sole approver, always. No delegation — DIY constraint means there's no one to hand off to. If Claude's script output needs heavy rewriting, either the scheduled-task prompt needs tuning (see `docs/marketing-plan.md` §6) or weekly volume drops further. Don't burn through the 3-hour budget on rewrites.

### Tools (actual working stack)

Claude API (scheduled task) → HeyGen Creator → Descript → Gamma → Later → ManyChat → Carrd → MailerLite. **No CapCut.** Total tooling ~$76/mo. DIY labour — no freelancers, editors, or agency.

## 9. Paid Social Strategy

See `docs/marketing-plan.md` Section 5 for full ad creative strategy. Strategic summary below.

### Pre-launch (Weeks 3–5)
- **Budget:** $10–15/day × 14 days = **$140–210**
- **Format:** Meta Lead Gen (native form, in-Instagram)
- **Creative:** Boost top-performing organic Reel (requires ~5 days of organic data first)
- **Targeting:** 40–65, fitness + Apple Watch + running + strength + longevity interests, UK/US/AU/CA
- **Goal:** Waitlist CPA < $2

### Launch week
- **Budget:** $30–50/day × 5 days = **$150–250**
- **Format:** App Install ads + retargeting waitlist as lookalike seed
- **Creative:** A/B test launch Reel vs. founder-story Reel
- **Goal:** Install CPA < $5, review Day 3 + reallocate

### Post-launch steady state (M1–M6)
- **Budget:** $300–1,000/month scaling with retention data
- **Format:** Mix of App Install (cold), retargeting (warm website visitors), lookalike of paying users (best)
- **Rule:** Only scale paid once organic M1→M2 follower growth is >20% month-over-month. Organic must carry the flywheel; paid is multiplier, not primary.

### Post-launch growth phase (M6–M12)
- **Budget target:** $3–10k/month depending on LTV:CAC ratios
- **Add:** TikTok Ads once TikTok organic reach > 10k/month consistently
- **Never:** More than 50% of new-user acquisition from paid. If it goes higher, stop and rebuild organic.

## 10. Metrics & Measurement

See `docs/social/metrics-dashboard.md` for the full weekly scorecard, review cadence, and "what to do when a metric is red" decision tree.

**Headline metrics (monthly review):**
- IG follower growth MoM (target: >25% MoM pre-launch, >40% M1–M3, >20% M3–M6)
- Reel reach (median, not average — one viral skews the average)
- Save rate per post (saves ÷ reach — proxy for genuine value)
- Waitlist → App Store install conversion
- DM → install conversion (ManyChat → App Store attribution via promo code)
- CAC from paid vs. blended CAC
- 30-day retention of socially-acquired users vs. paid users

**Review cadence:**
- **Weekly (15 min, Monday AM):** Top/bottom Reel of the week, what worked, what to stop doing
- **Monthly (60 min, first Monday):** Full dashboard, goal progress, reallocate budget, kill/double-down decisions
- **Quarterly (half day):** Strategy review — are pillars still working, platform mix still right, any new channel to test

## ⚠️ Calibration note

Targets below are rescaled to Kulvinder's **actual 3-hour-per-week** marketing time budget (parent + FT day job, DIY, no outside hires) plus **~$500 paid ads through launch**. The first version of this doc assumed 10–12 hrs/week of founder time; reality is a third of that. At the actual budget, paid ads do the work that organic engagement can't.

Actual baseline from Weeks 1–3 pre-launch:
- 5 Reels + 2 Carousels shipped (vs 15 + 6 planned) → **~33% of planned content shipping**
- Median Reel reach: **148**
- IG followers: 12 (started at 0)
- IG reach-to-follow conversion: ~1.2% (healthy benchmark 2–5%)
- Waitlist: ~5

This is not catastrophic for a new account — it's just what 3 hrs/week without engagement groundwork actually produces.

- As revenue comes in and time/budget flexibility improves, paid share drops naturally (M3+)
- The risk to watch: if paid share stays >50% past M3, it means organic hasn't started working and the plan needs revisiting

**Rule (revised):** paid share can legitimately exceed 50% pre-launch and M1–M2. From M3 onward, if it's still above 50%, review whether the time constraint has genuinely prevented organic from compounding — and whether that's now the actual bottleneck worth paying to solve.

## Section 4 — Decision Trees

"If this metric is red, do this. If it's red for 3 weeks running, do that."

### 4.1 If follower growth stalls (not hitting weekly target for 2+ weeks)

**Diagnose first:**
- Is the Reel reaching non-followers? Check IG Insights → % reach from non-followers on top posts. If <40%, we have a hook problem.
- Is engagement rate healthy but reach low? Shadow-ban unlikely but possible — check if hashtags are being suppressed (search own hashtag while logged out).

**Actions in order:**
1. **Week 1 red:** note it, don't change anything — wait one more week. Natural variance.
2. **Week 2 red:** diagnose. Pick the most likely cause (usually hook is weak). Rewrite next week's Reel hooks from scratch (don't let Claude just rotate templates — write 5 bespoke hooks manually).
3. **Week 3 red:** pause paid ads if running (they're masking an organic problem). Post a founder-voice "I've been thinking about X" Reel — genuine founder moments break the pattern. Ask the community directly via Story: "What do you want more of?"
4. **Week 4+ red:** rethink pillar mix. Most likely problem: too much product content. Pull Product Teasers back to 5%. Lean Wake-Up Call + Science to 60% combined.

### 4.2 If save rate drops below threshold (3–5% depending on phase)

**This is the most important red flag on the whole dashboard.** Save rate falling = content is no longer useful to the viewer.

Actions:
1. **Audit last 10 Reels:** which had saves >5%? What's the common thread? Almost always: specific, evidence-led, practically actionable. Save-hostile content is usually: personality-led without a takeaway, promotional without an education hook, long rambles without structure.
2. **Cut the weakest 20%:** remove whichever pillar is consistently under-performing on saves. (Through M3, this is usually Product Teasers — fine.)
3. **Double the strongest pillar:** whatever's saving best, make more of it for 2 weeks. Don't fear being repetitive — audience memory is shorter than you think.
4. **Test format:** if Reels are low-save but Carousels high, do more Carousels. Fitness-after-40 demo often saves Carousels more than Reels — let the data lead.

### 4.3 If CPA spikes above target (paid)

1. **Creative fatigue check (most common):** has this creative run >2 weeks? Refresh.
2. **Audience saturation:** is reach vs. frequency showing frequency >3? Expand audience or rotate.
3. **Landing page issue:** is waitlist form still working? Carrd load time OK? Check MailerLite integration.
4. **Wrong CTA:** too many CTAs in creative? Strip to one.
5. **If still red after 7 days of fixes:** pause paid for a week. Audit organic. Paid amplifies organic — it can't rescue it.

### 4.4 If DM response times slip past 4h consistently

This is the hire-a-community-manager signal. See `engagement-playbook.md` Section 7 for trigger criteria and profile.

### 4.5 If a Reel goes genuinely viral (>10x median reach)

1. **Don't over-capitalise immediately.** The hook that worked will not work twice. Don't re-post it as a Carousel this week.
2. **Do milk the distribution while it's live:**
   - Pin to profile for 2 weeks
   - Reply to every single comment personally within 24h of the spike (signals algorithm)
   - Update bio / link-in-bio to push whatever the Reel pointed at (waitlist / app)
   - Cross-post to TikTok (if not already) — often the same content works there with lag
3. **Debrief properly:** write in the weekly review doc what specifically made it work. Was it the hook? The format? Timing? An external trigger? Generalise to one actionable insight for future Reels.
4. **Paid-boost opportunity:** if CPA is good, this is the creative to boost. Run as ad for 10–14 days with $20–30/day on top of organic.

## Section 5 — Reporting templates

### 5.1 Monthly report (first Monday of each month — 60 min)

**Structure (5 sections, 2 pages max):**

1. **Headline number:** "Fitovo social grew IG followers 38% MoM (1,100 → 1,520). Top Reel reached 42,000 people — 3.2x the previous best."

2. **Scorecard vs. targets** — table of the metrics from Section 2.1–2.5, with 🟢/🟡/🔴 traffic lights.

3. **What worked:** 3 bullets. Specific. "Switched to 'named study + actionable outcome' hook structure — top 3 Reels all used it."

4. **What didn't:** 3 bullets. Honest. "Product teaser Carousels underperformed — only 2% save rate vs 5.5% average. Pulling back to 1/mo."

5. **Next month's plan:** 3 bullets. One change, one double-down, one experiment. "Experiment: test LinkedIn founder series. Double-down: HRV content. Change: cut product teasers to 1 per 2 weeks."

**Audience:** Kulvinder only (for now). Becomes investor / advisor update at M6+.

### 5.2 Quarterly strategic review (first week of quarter — half day)

**Four questions to answer:**

1. Are the pillars still the right pillars? (Check save rate by pillar over the quarter — kill any pillar <3% save rate for 2 months.)
2. Is the platform mix still right? (Check follower + engagement growth by platform. Any platform flatlining? Cut effort by half and redeploy.)
3. Are we hitting the one goal that matters most right now? (Pre-launch: waitlist. M1–M3: paying-user growth. M3+: retention.)
4. What's the biggest single lever for next quarter? (One thing.)

**Output:** 1-page memo, updates to `docs/social/strategy.md` in the relevant sections.

## Section 7 — Appendix: metric definitions

- **Reach:** unique accounts that saw the post (IG Insights).
- **Impressions:** total views (can double-count). We don't optimise for this — reach is honest.
- **Engagement rate:** (likes + comments + saves + shares) / reach × 100. We use reach not followers as denominator — more honest as audience grows.
- **Save rate:** saves / reach × 100. Headline value metric.
- **Median vs. average:** always prefer median Reel reach. One viral can 10x an average; median tells the truth about baseline performance.
- **Waitlist CPA:** paid ad spend / unique waitlist signups attributed to paid (via promo code / UTM / MailerLite source).
- **CAC:** total marketing spend / new paying users in period. Different to CPA — CAC includes tools, salary (eventually), everything.
- **LTV:** average revenue per paying user × predicted retention months. For subscription apps, start with a rough assumption (e.g., $8 ARPU × 10 months = $80 LTV) and refine quarterly.
- **LTV:CAC ratio:** >3:1 is healthy. Below 2:1 means cut spend or fix retention. We won't have real numbers until M3+.

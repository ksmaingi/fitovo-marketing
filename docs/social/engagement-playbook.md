# Fitovo Engagement & Community Playbook

**Owner:** Kulvinder (solo, DIY, 3 hrs/week total across all marketing)
**Cadence:** daily (10 min) · weekly (30 min production) · monthly rituals (batched)
**North-star principle:** *Every reply is a product decision.* Adults over 40 decide whether to trust you in a single interaction. The algorithm rewards replies; humans remember them.

---

## The binding constraint: 3 hours/week total

The first version of this playbook prescribed 60–90 min/day of founder engagement. That's not available. Kulvinder is a parent with a full-time other job; marketing work has to fit in ~3 hrs/week *including* content production.

So the engagement budget, honestly, is:

- **Content production: ~1h 30m/week** (Mon 30 min + Tue 30 min + Sun 15 min review + buffer)
- **Engagement: ~60 min/week** (10 min × 5 weekdays + buffer)
- **Total: ~2h 30m + 30 min buffer**

This playbook is scaled to that reality. Volume is lower than industry benchmark; **quality per interaction has to be higher to compensate**.

---

## 1. Daily 10-minute block (weekdays only)

One block per weekday. Mornings (commute, first coffee) or evenings (after kid bedtime) — whichever sticks.

| Min | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | Post today's Story (see §4). Phone photo + text overlay, or a reposted Reel clip, or a poll sticker. Don't overproduce. |
| 2–5 | Reply to any new comments on your own posts. If the comment is a question, answer usefully. If it's a ManyChat trigger, let the bot handle the DM and just drop a "Sent the link — anything specific you want to know?" reply. |
| 5–10 | Drop **3–5 thoughtful comments** on target accounts (see §2). Not "🔥". Not "great post." A specific observation, a data point, a genuine question. |

That's it. 10 min × 5 weekdays = **50 min/week** of community presence.

### If you have 3 min (sick / travel day)
- Reply to any new comments on your posts. Skip the Story. Skip proactive commenting.

### If you have 15 min
- The above + 5 more proactive comments.

## 2. Target account list (keep small)

The first version of this playbook tried to track 30 rolling accounts. That's too many for 5 daily comments. Simplify to a **rotating set of 10 accounts** Kulvinder actually engages with weekly.

**Pick 10** from across these tiers, update the list monthly:

- **3 industry experts** — Dr. Vonda Wright, Stacy Sims, Gabrielle Lyon, Mary Claire Haver, Andy Galpin, Peter Attia (pick any 3, rotate)
- **4 mid-tier creators** in the 40+ fitness niche (see `influencer-targets.md` Tier 2 for starter list)
- **3 micro creators / potential users** — actual adults 40-65 posting about their training, HRV, recovery. Scout weekly via `#fitnessafter40` `#fitnessafter50` `#longevitytraining` hashtags.

Follow them. Turn on post notifications for 3 of them. That way new-content shows up without scouting.

### What "good" comments look like

✅ **Do:**
- Reference a specific claim or moment ("Your point about RHR drift at 52 matched exactly what our users are seeing…")
- Add data ("The Lancet study you mentioned — they found X. Matches the 5 bpm threshold.")
- Ask a genuinely curious question ("Have you tried this with clients who don't have a Watch yet? We keep hearing that's the gap.")

❌ **Don't:**
- Generic emojis or "Great post!"
- Pitch Fitovo in a comment — ever
- Correct them publicly even when they're wrong (take to DM)
- Copy-paste the same comment across multiple posts

## 3. Reactive engagement — realistic response targets

These are scaled to a 3-hr/week budget. Industry "best practice" (reply within 2 h) doesn't apply when the founder has a day job.

| Type | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Comments on your posts | reply within **24 h** | First 24 h of a post is the algorithm-signal window, so check your daily block falls inside it |
| Mentions / tags | reply within **24 h** business day | Acknowledge + reshare if positive |
| DMs (non-ManyChat automated) | reply within **24 h** | ManyChat handles the waitlist-trigger flow automatically |
| ManyChat-triggered DMs | automated | Just verify the bot is still working weekly (check in ManyChat) |
| Negative feedback | **acknowledge within 24 h**, move to DM | Full protocol below |

**Why 24 h not 2 h:** you're reviewing comments once daily in your 10-min block. That means worst-case reply time is 24 h. This is fine — the audience is 40-65 professionals, not teenagers expecting instant response. Authenticity beats speed.

### Comment-type tiering (during your 2–5 min reply block)

| Comment type | Response | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Question | Answer in detail. Free thought leadership for next viewer. | "Does readiness mode work without a Watch?" → "Yes — manual mode lets you rate sleep + energy. Readiness still works, just from your inputs." |
| Trigger word (ManyChat) | Bot DMs first. Then short reply to comment. | "READY" → [bot DMs link] → you reply: "Sent the link!" |
| Personal story | Acknowledge the *specific* detail, don't generalise. | "Just turned 55 and blew out my knee" → "Knee rehab after 50 is its own beast — tell me what your PT has you doing and I'll share some reading" |
| Disagreement (genuine) | Engage respectfully. Concede what's fair. Never "well actually." | "Norwegian 4x4 is overrated" → "Fair — mortality data is actually better on Z2 consistency than 4x4 intensity. What's worked for you?" |
| Disagreement (bad-faith) | Reply once, politely + data. If they escalate, silence. | One reply, then move on. |
| Praise / thanks | Short, warm, personal. Don't just 🙏. | "This made my morning" → "Appreciate you. What hit hardest?" |
| Tag of a friend | Welcome both people if possible. | "@friend you need this" → "Welcome [friend's name] — anything you want to know?" |

### Negative feedback protocol

Same as before — just slower response clock:
- Public comment: "App crashed, refund me"
- Public reply (within 24 h): "I'm sorry that happened — sending you a DM now."
- DM: Troubleshoot + refund if needed.
- Public follow-up comment (24–72 h later): "Got this resolved — thanks for the patience."

**Never:**
- Argue publicly
- Delete negative comments (unless slurs / abuse — then block without drama)
- Go silent (worse than an imperfect reply)

## 4. Stories — 5/week, lightweight only

The first draft had 21 Stories/week. Not possible at this time budget. Scale to **1 Story per weekday**, 2 min production each, phone only.

### Weekly Story theme (simple rotation)

| Day | Theme | Format (2 min max) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | "Week's intention" | Question sticker: "One thing you're working on this week?" |
| **Tue** | Tuesday's Reel re-share | Share Reel to Story with "New Reel ↑" text overlay |
| **Wed** | Quick tip | Text overlay with one actionable tip from this week's content |
| **Thu** | Poll | Pillar-relevant quick poll (4 options max) |
| **Fri** | Friday's Reel re-share + "Win of the week" | Share Reel, then question sticker: "Win this week — training or otherwise?" |

Weekend Stories: skip. If inspired Saturday morning, post one. Otherwise rest. The 5 weekday Stories are what matter.

### What NOT to build

Any of these are time traps and should be skipped:
- Behind-the-scenes videos that require recording + editing
- Multi-slide Story threads that take >5 min to produce
- Anything that needs a separate app (unless it's Later)

## 5. Monthly rituals (batched, not weekly)

Industry advice says "monthly Live, monthly Spotlight, monthly retrospective." At 3 hrs/week, all three is impossible monthly. Pick **one per month** and rotate:

| Month | Ritual (pick one) | Budget |
|---|---|---|
| Odd months (Jan/Mar/May/Jul/Sep/Nov) | **Instagram Live Q&A**, 20 min, first Friday. Lightweight — take 3 pre-submitted questions via Story question-box the week before. Record, clip 1 moment for a Reel next week. | 30 min prep + 20 min live = ~1 hr |
| Even months (Feb/Apr/Jun/Aug/Oct/Dec) | **"What I learned this month" Reel** or Carousel, last week of the month. Single piece of content. | 30 min extra on top of normal weekly cadence |

Member Spotlights move to **quarterly** (not monthly) — one per quarter, planned in advance, recruited with the user's explicit consent and a photo they provide.

### Quarterly rituals
- **End of quarter:** one Member Spotlight Carousel (Gamma) + one "how the community is doing" Reel. Batched across a long weekend afternoon.
- **Launch anniversary (annual, May 2027):** community retrospective — plan 4 weeks in advance.

## 7. Crisis & escalation

Unchanged from the first draft. The rules don't bend based on time budget.

- Tier 1 (single unhappy user): standard protocol, handle solo within 24 h
- Tier 2 (3+ similar complaints in 24 h): pause paid ads, Story acknowledgement within 4 h, fix or communicate fix timeline publicly
- Tier 3 (viral / PR): stop scheduled content; holding Story within 2 h; don't freestyle — draft, sleep on it, ship
- Tier 4 (medical claims): contact lawyer before response; Fitovo is not medical advice, that must be clear in product and content

Speed on crisis is non-negotiable. If something breaks publicly during an unavailable period (weekend, deep work), check in — crisis is the one exception to the "no weekend engagement" rule.

## 8. The KPI that matters most

Same as before — **saves per Reel ÷ reach**. Target >5% M1–3, >3% ongoing.

At 2 Reels/week, save rate per Reel is the single most reliable quality signal. If it drops below threshold for 2 weeks:
1. Don't post more to compensate. Post less.
2. Kill the weakest-performing pillar for a month. (Through M3 this is usually Product Teasers — fine.)
3. Double down on whichever pillar saves best (usually Science or Actionable).

## 9. What we're *not* doing (deliberately)

To be honest with ourselves:

- ❌ Not replying in 2 h. 24 h is the standard.
- ❌ Not commenting on 30 posts/day. 3–5 per weekday. 25/week total.
- ❌ Not shipping daily Stories on weekends.
- ❌ Not running weekly Lives. Monthly at most.
- ❌ Not hiring a community manager. DIY forever unless the constraint changes.
- ❌ Not doing Member Spotlights monthly. Quarterly.
- ❌ Not doing the Tier 3 micro-creator outreach until the app is live and there's something to give them.

This is the plan that fits. Executing half of it well beats executing all of the first draft poorly.
